Me in The Army

January 19, 2018 - 2 minute read -

Short Sad Story

I graduated in July 2016 then after five months doing nothing except waiting to get exempted from conscription (Don’t judge I was optimistic back then), But sadly in 19th of Jan 2017 I became another solider in the Egyptian army.

I finished my training in 26 days, then I joined my unit in 6th of March. It was located in Suez. I’d been assigned to write documents on a computer. It was an easy job, but I didn’t get comfortable working under noncommissioned officers. Luckily After a week I found an officer looking for soldiers with programming experience. I looked into it and discovered he was making soldiers developing Windows desktop applications using C#, Winforms and MSSQL. Despite my zero knowledge of them, I knew this was my lucky ticket to escape from my old job. I considered the trade offs, studied C# basics and finally applied. They asked me to write a program to calculate the sum of two numbers. I was like “Are you kidding me!”. I solved it and got in. I know that you think it was a trivial test. You would be surprised if you knew how many people failed in it. If it makes you happy I leveled up the task to write a program to check whether the number even or odd.

As a start I wrote a script to countdown my time in the army and It was like hell. Nothing slows down the time like looking at it, but I couldn’t resist.

I spend most of the time developing CRUD desktop apps, generating reports, writing reusable modules to save me time and finally fighting bugs. That’s it, my job was to computerize the workflow in the unit.

Finally

It was like any other period in my life has its ups and downs. I consider myself lucky for practicing programming all this year and meeting new awesome friends I couldn’t imagine what this year could be without them. I better now let them go because they know too much.
